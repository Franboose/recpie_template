Oha Soup

Oha soup, or ofe ora as the Indigenous Ibo people of Nigeria call it,
is packed with protein and a copious amount of leafy greens.
For a filling meal, enjoy it with a smooth log of pounded yam or fufu.

  • Preparation Time: 1 hour
  • Cooking Time: 1 hour 30 minutes
  • Serves: 6 people

Ingredients

  • Cocoa yam see the video below(about 15 to 20 medium sizes)
  • 2 kg Goat meat
  • 200 g Dry fish or mangala
  • 3 seasoning cubes
  • 1 cup ground crayfishh
  • 150 g Stockfish head
  • 300 ml Palm oil
  • 4 tbsp of ofor or achi as an alternative thickener
  • 1 tbsp Ogiri local ingredients
  • salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups of water or meat stock
Instructions
  • You want to start by cooking and pounding the coco yams,
    Just wash with water and boil for 15-20 minutes.
  • Peel off the back and pound using a mortar and pestle.
  • Slice the oha leaves and pound or grind the peppers and crayfish. Set aside.
  • Wash the dried fish and stockfish with hot water and tear them into smaller bits.
  • Add the oha leaves and stir. Cook for another 3 minutes.
  • Precook the goeat meat using a teaspoon of salt and 2 seasoning cubes.
  • Add 5 cups of water to the cooked meat, then 300 ml (palm oil),
    fish and stockfish, the ground crayfish and 1 seasoning cube.
  • Add salt and pepper to taste.
  • Stir your soup and allow boiling for 10 minutes.
  • Add the pounded coco yam and ogiri stir. Cook for another 10 minutes.
  • Add the sliced oha leaves. Allow to simmer for 4 minutes.
  • Serve hot with pounded yam or fufu.
